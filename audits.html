// THE MARKET THIS SERVES

Enterprise security firms are priced out of the small business market. The organizations that need security assessments most — independent insurance brokers, logistics operations, medical practices, small law firms — cannot afford $50,000 penetration tests. Their current security posture is somewhere between "nothing formal" and "a checklist someone made in 2019." That is a large and underserved market. The forge serves it.

// WHAT AN AUDIT COVERS
DATA HANDLING PRACTICES

How sensitive data is collected, stored, transmitted, and deleted. Where the gaps are between current practice and reasonable compliance. What the exposure looks like if a breach occurs.

NETWORK ARCHITECTURE REVIEW

External attack surface, internal network segmentation, wireless security, remote access configuration, and firewall rules. Passive assessment — no disruption to operations.

DEVICE MANAGEMENT POSTURE

Endpoint inventory, patch management practices, mobile device policies, and printer/IoT device exposure. The devices that nobody thinks about are usually the most exposed.

SOFTWARE SUPPLY CHAIN

Third-party vendor assessment, SaaS permission review, API key management, and software update practices. Who has access to your systems that you have not thought about recently.

WEB-FACING INFRASTRUCTURE

Basic penetration testing for externally-facing systems. Common vulnerability classes, SSL/TLS configuration, authentication posture, and public exposure assessment.

SOCIAL ENGINEERING EXPOSURE

Phishing susceptibility assessment, password policy review, and employee security awareness baseline. The human layer is usually where breaches begin.

// WHAT YOU RECEIVE
EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Leadership-readable overview of findings, risk level, and priority actions. Written for the person who makes decisions, not the person who manages servers.

TECHNICAL FINDINGS

Detailed findings for whoever manages your systems. Specific vulnerabilities, affected systems, severity ratings, and exact remediation steps.

REMEDIATION PRIORITY MATRIX

Everything ranked by severity and effort. What to fix first, what to fix next, and what to monitor. A roadmap, not just a list of problems.

FOLLOW-UP CALL

A call to walk through results, answer questions, and confirm the remediation plan makes sense for your specific situation and resources.

// WHAT THIS IS NOT

The forge is not a large penetration testing firm. Red team engagements for enterprise environments are not on the menu. What is on the menu: rigorous, honest security assessments for organizations whose current security posture needs a baseline review. If the scope of your need exceeds what is offered here, that will be stated in the discovery call — along with a referral if appropriate.

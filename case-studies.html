Pisces Moon OS — v1.0.0 "THE ARSENAL"

The first known documented implementation of persistent dual-core background tasking for field intelligence collection on the ESP32-S3 hardware class. Built on a $50 LilyGO T-Deck Plus. 47 applications across 7 categories. Two primary inventions:

The Ghost Engine — a persistent Core 0 process that wardrives, scans BLE, and logs GPS continuously, always, regardless of what the operator is doing on Core 1. The device is always collecting. The operator is never interrupted.

The SPI Bus Treaty — the first named architectural standard for shared-bus arbitration on the ESP32-S3. Now part of the public technical literature. Historical lineage: Unix filesystem locking (1970s), Apollo AGC priority scheduling (1969), N64 RSP time budget (1996).

Six engineering problems were encountered and solved — none had prior documented solutions for this hardware platform. DEF CON 34 CFP submitted. Submission ID: 1349.
